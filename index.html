Diamonds

How to Buy a Diamond.

The four ‘c’s for a Diamond, colour, clarity, cut and carat are important but unless you have some expertise, not overly relevant.

The best Diamond is a colour ‘D’, clarity ‘IF’ (Internally Flawless), excellent cut and whatever size you like! A 1.0 carat Diamond is a lot more expensive than a comparable 0.9 carat gem and only an expert would be able to tell the difference in size when it is set in a ring.

If you have $10 000 to spend, here is what you will get:

Colour Carat Clarity
D 0.5 SI (Slightly Included)
E/F 0.8 SI
G/H 1.0 SI

The best way to buy a Diamond is simply decide on your budget and how large a Diamond you want, then the rest just follows on. Here is a simple exercise, go to any ‘Proper’ jeweller and ask them to show you some loose Diamonds in the price range you want. They should explain how the ‘Colours’ and clarity vary and you’ll soon see how important the colour is.

NEVER BUY A DIAMOND:

  1. That has not been tested by a reputable laboratory.
  2. Whose colour is ‘I’, or worse...they are BROWN!
  3. A ‘Champagne’ Diamond...they are BROWN!
  4. That does not ‘Sparkle’. The sparkle means that it was well cut, a dull gem is probably poor quality and poorly cut.
  5. In Australia. It is sad but true that Diamonds are cheaper in Thailand.
